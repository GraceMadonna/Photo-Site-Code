A Little Bit About Me

Hello! My name is Grace Madonna and I'm a Massachusetts based photographer. I mostly photograph portraits, but I also enjoy photographing nature and landscapes. If you are interested in setting up a shoot, you can take a look at my booking information. Check out my work in the portfolio page!

My Journey

October 2014

When I was a tiny 13 year old, I was waiting to be picked up at the mall during sunset and thought it was so beautiful that I had to capture it. I pulled out my iTouch and snapped these three photos.

These photos sparked a passion in me that I didn't know I had, jumpstarting everything I am today. Immediately I began taking pictures of everything around me with that iTouch and putting terrible filters on all of them. After some time, my parents saw that I was committed and actually wanted to stick with this passion, which was something I generally didn't do as a kid. And after a couple months of begging, I got my first camera!

January 2015

We did hours of research and came to the conclusion and made the decision to get a cute little black Fuji Film that I used to take pictures of everything while sitting in the backseat of my parents' cars. I took a local photography class to learn how to use all the different settings and buttons and fell in love with the endless possibilities.

December 2015

Nearly a year had passed of me capturing everything in my vision, and Christmas was coming up, and I knew the only thing a wanted was a real professional camera. After looking at a consumer reports magazine, we decided that the Nikon D3300 had the the best deal for it's price. On Christmas morning I opened up my beautiful new camera and got so excited that I shoved the battery in backwards. I now had to wait three days to actually use it becuase we had to send it to a shop to fix it for me. I was pretty bummed about that. But then I got it back! And I took a bunch of photos of my lovely dog, Mazi. They were way too dark and completely unedited, but I was thrilled with them at the time.

December 2015 - June 2018

Once I got my current camera, I used it a lot! I typically took breaks over the winter, then would pick it up again when it got warm out, but I loved it. I edited with some crappy phone apps that had hundreds of filters that I downloaded for free. They were technically called video LUTs, actually. My instagram was a mess with nothing making it look cohesive, but I was learning to find my style.

July 2018 and December 2018

For my 17th birthday and that year's Christmas, I was dead set on getting everything I could to improve my photography. I wanted lightroom more than anything for better editing and decided I needed a prism and rainbow catcher. I got lightroom early, before my birthday, so I could use it to edit photos from when the family went to Colorado. I downloaded a bunch of free presets that after about a week, I never touched again. Then I got to play with my prism after opening it on Christmas! They both changed my life.

Today

Now I am doing more photoshoots than ever and improving with every single one. Everywhere I look, I think about how my current location or a place on tv would look in a photo. My world is through a lens and I'd love it if you'd allow me the pleasure of capturing you in it!
